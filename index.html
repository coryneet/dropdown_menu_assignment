Houseplant Care Guide

Houseplants bring life and freshness to any indoor space, but proper care is essential to keep them thriving. Watering is a critical factor—most plants like pothos prefer their soil to dry out slightly between waterings, typically every 1-2 weeks depending on light and humidity. Light needs vary: low-light plants like snake plants can handle shady corners, while monstera thrives in bright, indirect sunlight. Humidity is another key element, especially for tropical species—consider using a pebble tray or humidifier if your home is dry.

Fertilizing should be done sparingly, about once a month during the growing season with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer. Repotting becomes necessary when roots outgrow their pots, usually every 1-2 years, and it’s a great opportunity to refresh the soil. Pest control is vital too—watch for spider mites or aphids and treat with neem oil or insecticidal soap. Regularly cleaning leaves with a damp cloth removes dust, helping plants photosynthesize efficiently. With these tips, your houseplants can flourish year-round.

Tip: Observe your plants closely—yellowing leaves might indicate overwatering, while brown tips suggest low humidity. Adjust care as needed!
